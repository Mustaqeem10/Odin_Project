Cherry Dump Cake

Description

A cherry dump cake is a simple dessert recipe that is made by dumping cans of cherry pie filling into a baking dish, topping it with a boxed cake mix and butter. The result is an ultra moist, fruity, and slightly crumbly cake that’s impossible to resist.

A dump cake is a dessert that’s made by dumping a variety of ingredients into a baking dish and then baking it. The end result is a cake-like dessert with a variety of flavors and textures.

The most common ingredients that are used in dump cakes are canned fruit (such as pineapples, peaches, or cherries), cake mix, butter, and nuts. However, variations on the recipe are endless, so feel free to get creative!

Ingredients

  • 2 (21 ounce) cans cherry pie filling
  • 1 (15.25 ounce) package white cake mix
  • ¼ cup butter, melted
  • ½ cup coarsely chopped pecans

Steps

  1. Preheat the oven to 350 degrees F (175 degrees C).
  2. Spread cherry pie filling over the bottom of a 9x13-inch baking pan. Stir cake mix and butter together into a gooey, clumpy mixture. Break into pieces between your fingers and crumble evenly on top of the cherry filling. Sprinkle pecans on top.
  3. Bake in the preheated oven until golden brown on top, 35 to 40 minutes.
